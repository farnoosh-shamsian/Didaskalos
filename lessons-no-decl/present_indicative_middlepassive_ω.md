# Lesson: Present‑Indicative Middle/Passive (‑ω‑) Forms  

*Target level: Beginner‑Intermediate*  
*Prerequisite: Greek alphabet, basic idea of verb stems and the present‑indicative active endings.*

---

## 1. What the Present‑Indicative Middle/Passive Is  

In Ancient Greek every finite verb has **voice** (active, middle, passive) and **tense‑mood‑person**.  
The **present indicative** tells us that an action is occurring **now** (or habitually) and that the statement is **factual**.  

The **middle** and **passive** share the same set of endings in the present indicative.  
- **Middle** – the subject is somehow involved in the action (reflexive, reciprocal, or “for its own benefit”).  
- **Passive** – the subject is acted upon by someone else.  

Both voices are built on the **thematic vowel –ο‑ (omega)**, which is why the paradigm is often labelled “‑ω‑”.  
The first‑person singular middle/passive form ends in **‑ομαι**, not in the active **‑ω**, but the vowel **‑ο‑** is the same element that appears in the active ending **‑ω** (e.g. λύω “I loose” vs. λύομαι “I am loosed / I loose for myself”).

---

## 2. Forming the Present‑Indicative Middle/Passive  

### 2.1 General pattern  

| Person | Ending (middle/passive) | Example with **λύω** (to loose) |
|--------|------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| 1 sg   | ‑ **ομαι**             | λύ**ομαι** – “I am loosed / I loose for myself” |
| 2 sg   | ‑ **σαι**              | λύ**εσαι** – “you are loosed / you loose for yourself” |
| 3 sg   | ‑ **ται**              | λύ**εται** – “he/she/it is loosed / looses for himself” |
| 1 pl   | ‑ **ομεθα**            | λύ**ομεθα** – “we are loosed / we loose for ourselves” |
| 2 pl   | ‑ **εσθε**             | λύ**εσθε** – “you (pl.) are loosed / you loose for yourselves” |
| 3 pl   | ‑ **ονται**            | λύ**ονται** – “they are loosed / they loose for themselves” |

**How to build the form**

1. **Find the present‑stem** (the same stem used for the active present).  
   - For regular verbs: remove the active ending ‑ω from the 1 sg active.  
   - Example: λύω → stem **λυ‑**.  
2. **Add the thematic vowel –ο‑** (sometimes contracted, see § 3).  
3. **Attach the appropriate middle/passive ending** from the table above.

### 2.2 Contract verbs  

Many verbs end in **‑εω, ‑οω, ‑αω** in the active. Their middle/passive forms contract the thematic –ο‑ with the final vowel of the stem.

| Active infinitive | Middle/Passive 1 sg | Explanation |
|-------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **φιλέω** “to love” | **φιλόμαι** | ε + ο → **ο** (no lengthening) |
| **παίω** “to strike” | **παίομαι** | No contraction; the stem already ends in a vowel |
| **διδώ** “to give” (verb **διδώ** is irregular) | **διδούμαι** | ο + ω → **ου** (spurious diphthong) |
| **βασιλεύω** “to be king” | **βασιλεύομαι** | No contraction; the stem ends in ‑ευ‑ |

**Typical contraction patterns**

| Stem ending | +ο‑ → Result | Example |
|-------------|------------|---------|
| ‑ε   | ‑ε + ο → **‑ο** | φιλέω → φιλόμαι |
| ‑ο   | ‑ο + ο → **‑ου** (spurious diphthong) | διδώ → διδούμαι |
| ‑α   | ‑α + ο → **‑αο** → **‑α** (long α) | τιμάω → τιμῶ (active) → τιμῶμαι (middle) |

## 3. Full Paradigm for a Regular Verb  

Below is the complete present‑indicative active vs. middle/passive for the regular verb **λύω** “to loose”.

|               | **Active** | **Middle/Passive** |
|---------------|------------|--------------------|
| **1 sg**      | λύ**ω**   | λύ**ομαι** |
| **2 sg**      | λύ**εις**| λύ**εσαι** |
| **3 sg**      | λύ**ει** | λύ**εται** |
| **1 pl**      | λύ**ομεν**| λύ**ομεθα** |
| **2 pl**      | λύ**ετε**| λύ**εσθε** |
| **3 pl**      | λύ**ουσι(ν)**| λύ**ονται** |

*Notice the vowel lengthening in the active 3 pl (‑ουσι) but not in the middle/passive (‑ονται).*

## 4. Key Rules & Common Exceptions  

| Rule | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **A. Thematic vowel –ο‑** | Insert –ο‑ before the ending. | λυ‑ + ο‑ + ‑μαι → λυομαι |
| **B. Contraction** | When the stem ends in a vowel, –ο‑ may contract (ε+ο → ο, ο+ο → ου, α+ο → α). | φιλέω → φιλόμαι; διδώ → διδούμαι |
| **C. Verbs ending in ‑μι** | These have irregular middle/passive forms (often ‑μαι). | τίθημι → τίθεμαι |
| **D. Deponent verbs** | Appear only in middle/passive forms, but have active meaning. | ἔρχομαι “I come” (no active λύω counterpart) |
| **E. Irregular verbs** | Some common verbs have special stems. | εἰμί → εἰμί (active) but εἰσ-? (no middle); ἔχω → ἔχομαι (middle) |
| **F. Passive of verbs with a‑stem** | The passive often uses a different stem (e.g., *γράφω* → *γράφεται*). | γράφ‑ + ο‑ + ‑εται → γράφεται |
| **G. The “‑σθαι” infinitive** | The present infinitive of the middle/passive is **‑σθαι** (e.g., λύεσθαι). | λύεσθαι “to be loosed / to loose oneself” |

## 5. Example Sentences  

| Greek (middle/passive) | Transliteration | English |
|------------------------|----------------|---------|
| **Λύομαι ἀπὸ τῆς ἀγγέλως.** | Lýomai apò tês angélôs. | “I am loosed from the pain.” |
| **Ἡ γυνὴ ἐπὶ τῇ ἀγορᾷ ἀγοράζεται.** | Hē gynē epì tē̂i agorâi agorázetai. | “The woman is buying (for herself) at the market.” |
| **Τὰ παιδία ἀγωνίζονται ἀλλήλων.** | Ta paidiá agōnízontai allḗlōn. | “The children compete with each other.” (reciprocal middle) |
| **Οἱ στρατιῶται ἐπὶ τὸν πόλεμον ἀποστέλλονται.** | Hoí stratiôtai epì tòn pólemon apostéllontai. | “The soldiers are being sent to the war.” |
| **Ἐγὼ τὸν νόμον τηρῶμαι.** | Egṓ tòn nómón tērômai. | “I keep (observe) the law (for my own benefit).” |

## 6. Usage & Context  

1. **Middle voice** is the default for many verbs that describe an action performed **for the subject’s own interest** (e.g., *πλέομαι* “I wash myself”).  
2. It also marks **reciprocal** actions (*ἀγωνίζεσθαι* “to compete with each other”).  
3. **Passive voice** is used when the subject is the **patient** of an external agent (*γράφεται* “it is written”).  
4. In **literary Greek** the middle often replaces a reflexive passive that later languages would express with a separate reflexive pronoun.  
5. **Deponent verbs** (e.g., *ἔρχομαι*, *ἀποκρίνομαι*) appear only in middle/passive forms but have active meaning; they must be memorised individually.
