# Lesson: The Aorist Indicative Passive (ending ‑ω)  

*Target level: Beginner‑Intermediate*  
*Prerequisites: Greek alphabet, basic verb concepts (person, number, voice, tense, mood).*

---

## 1. What is the Aorist Indicative Passive?

| Term | Meaning in Greek | English gloss |
|------|------------------|---------------|
| **Aorist** | Simple past (a “punctual” action) | “did / was …ed” |
| **Indicative** | The “statement” mood – used for factual statements | – |
| **Passive** | The subject receives the action rather than performs it | “was …ed” |
| **‑ω** | The first‑person singular ending of many aorist passive forms (e.g. *ἐδόθην* → “I was given”). In the full paradigm the ending appears as **‑θην**; the **‑ω** you see in dictionaries is the contracted form of **‑θην** after a vowel. |

The aorist indicative passive therefore tells us **that something happened to the subject, and it happened once or as a complete event**. It is the “simple past passive” of Ancient Greek.

---

## 2. Forming the Aorist Indicative Passive  

### 2.1 Regular (‑θην) pattern  

1. **Take the verb’s aorist stem** (often the same as the present stem, sometimes with a special aorist marker).  
2. **Add the passive suffix ‑θ‑** (the “thematic” aorist passive marker).  
3. **Add the personal endings** shown in the table below.

| Person | Ending | Example with *λῡ́ω* (to loosen) → *ἐλῡ́θην* |
|--------|--------|--------------------------------------------|
| 1st sg | ‑θην → ‑ω (contracted) | ἐλῡ́**ω** (I was loosed) |
| 2nd sg | ‑θης | ἐλῡ́**ης** (you were loosed) |
| 3rd sg | ‑θη | ἐλῡ́**η** (he/she/it was loosed) |
| 1st pl | ‑θημεν | ἐλῡ́**ημεν** (we were loosed) |
| 2nd pl | ‑θητε | ἐλῡ́**ητε** (you all were loosened) |
| 3rd pl | ‑θησαν | ἐλῡ́**ησαν** (they were loosed) |

> **Why the “‑ω”?**  
> In the first‑person singular the vowel of the stem + **‑θ** + **‑ην** contracts to **‑ω** (e.g., *‑αθ‑ην → ‑αω → ‑ω*). Dictionaries therefore list the form as *ἐλῡ́ω* even though the full analytic form is *ἐλῡ́θην*.

### 2.2 Irregular / Suppletive stems  

Some verbs have a completely different aorist passive stem. Memorise these as separate lexical items.

| Verb (infinitive) | Aorist passive stem | 1st sg form (‑ω) | Example translation |
|-------------------|---------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **διδάσκω** “to teach” | *διδασκ‑* → *διδασκ‑* + ‑θ‑ | *διδασκ**ε**ν* → *διδασκ**ε**ν* (no contraction) → *διδασκ**ε**ν* (rare) | “I was taught” |
| **δίδωμι** “to give” | *δο‑* | *δω**θ**ην* → *δω**θ**ην* → *δω**θ**ην* (contracted to *δω**θ**ην*) → *δω**θ**ην* (no ‑ω) |
| **ἔρχομαι** “to come” (aorist *ἦλθον*) | *ἐλθ‑* (perfective) | *ἐλήλθ**ην** → *ἐλήλθ**ην** (no ‑ω) | “I came (aorist) – passive not used” |

> **Tip:** For most regular verbs you will use the **‑θην** pattern; only a handful of common verbs are irregular.

---

## 3. Full Paradigm (Regular Verb)  

Below is the complete aorist indicative passive paradigm for a regular verb **λῡ́ω** “to loosen”. The same pattern applies to any verb that follows the regular ‑θ‑ passive formation.

|          | Singular | Plural |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **1st**  | ἐλῡ́**ω** (contracted from ἐλῡ́**θην**) | ἐλῡ́**ημεν** |
| **2nd**  | ἐλῡ́**ης** | ἐλῡ́**ητε** |
| **3rd**  | ἐλῡ́**η** | ἐλῡ́**ησαν** |

*Note:* The first‑person singular is the only form that shows the contracted **‑ω**. All other persons retain the full **‑θη‑** sequence.

---

## 4. Key Rules & Common Exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **A. Use ‑θ‑ for the regular aorist passive** | Attach ‑θ‑ to the aorist stem before the personal endings. | *γράφω* → aorist stem *γραψ‑* → *ἐγράψ‑θην* → *ἐγράψ**ω** (contracted)* |
| **B. Contract the first‑person singular** | Vowel + ‑θ‑ + ‑ην contracts to **‑ω**. | *παιδεύω* → *παιδεύ**θην** → *παιδεύ**ω*** |
| **C. Verbs with a “‑σ‑” before ‑θ‑ lose the sigma** | The sigma drops before the passive suffix. | *παιδεύω* → *παιδεύ**θην*** (no **σ**). |
| **D. Verbs whose aorist stem ends in a vowel** | The vowel + ‑θ‑ + ‑ην contracts directly to **‑ω**. | *ἔχω* → aorist stem *ἐσ‑* → *ἐσ**θην** → *ἐσ**ω*** (rare). |
| **E. Irregular stems** | Some verbs have suppletive aorist passive stems (e.g., *διδάσκω* → *διδασκ‑*). | *διδάσκω* → *διδασκ**θην*** (no contraction). |
| **F. No passive for deponent verbs** | Deponent verbs are middle‑voice in form but active in meaning; they do **not** form a passive. | *ἔρχομαι* has no aorist passive. |

## 5. Example Sentences  

| Greek (aorist passive) | Transliteration | English |
|------------------------|----------------|---------|
| 1. Ἡ πόλις **ἐκρατήθη** ὑπὸ τῶν Ἑλληνῶν. | Hē polis **ekratēthē** hypo tōn Hellēnōn. | The city **was captured** by the Greeks. |
| 2. Ὁ διδάσκαλος **ἐδιδάχθη** ὑπὸ τῶν μαθητῶν. | Ho didaskalos **edidachthē** hypo tōn mathētōn. | The teacher **was taught** by the students. |
| 3. Ἐγώ **ἐλῡ́ω** τὸν δεσμὸν ὑπὸ τῆς ἀρετῆς. | Egō **elūō** ton desmon hypo tēs aretēs. | I **was loosed** from the bond by virtue. |
| 4. Ἡ γυνὴ **ἐδόθη** τὸ δῶρον. | Hē gynē **edothē** to dōron. | The woman **was given** the gift. |
