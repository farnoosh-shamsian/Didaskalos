# Lesson: The Imperfect Indicative — Middle/Passive (‑ω)

*Target level: Beginner‑Intermediate*  
*Prerequisites: Greek alphabet, basic knowledge of present‑tense endings and the augment (ἐ‑).*

---

## 1. What the Imperfect Middle/Passive Means  

| Aspect | Meaning in English | Typical English equivalent |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------------|
| **Tense** | **Imperfect** – an action that was ongoing, repeated, or incomplete in the past. | “was doing”, “used to do”, “kept doing”. |
| **Mood** | **Indicative** – stating a fact (as opposed to a command, wish, etc.). |
| **Voice** | **Middle / Passive** – the subject is either acting on itself (middle) or is acted upon (passive). In the imperfect the middle and passive share the same set of endings. |
| **Form** | The stem is the **present stem** plus the **augment** (ἐ‑) and the **middle/passive imperfect endings**. The augment marks past time. |

> **Middle** often expresses a reflexive or “for one’s own benefit” sense (e.g., *ἔλυόμην* “I was loosening for myself”).  
> **Passive** expresses that the subject receives the action (e.g., *ἐλύετο* “it was being loosened”).

---

## 2. Building the Imperfect Middle/Passive

1. **Find the present stem** (the part that appears before the present‑tense endings –ο‑, –ε‑, –α‑, etc.).  
   *λῡ‑* from **λῡ́ω** “to loosen”, *παι‑* from **παίω** “to strike”, *ἔρχ‑* from **ἔρχομαι** “to come”.

2. **Add the augment**:  
   * If the stem begins with a consonant → prepend **ἐ‑** (e.g., *ἐ‑λῡ‑*).  
   * If the stem begins with a vowel → lengthen the initial vowel (α → ἠ, ε → ἠ, ι → ῑ, ο → ω, υ → ῡ).  
   * For **contract verbs** (e.g., *φιλέω*), the augment is added to the contracted form (e.g., *ἐφίλε* → *ἐφίλε‑*).

3. **Attach the middle/passive imperfect endings** (see the paradigm below).  

---

## 3. Full Paradigm (Regular Verbs)

Below the tables use the verb **λῡ́ω** “to loosen” as a model.  
The same endings apply to any regular verb whose present stem ends in a consonant; contract verbs and verbs ending in **‑μι** have minor adjustments (see § 4).
