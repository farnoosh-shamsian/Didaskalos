# Lesson: The Imperfect Indicative Active ( ‑ω )  

*Target level: Beginner‑Intermediate*  
*Prerequisites: Greek alphabet, basic idea of verb stems and present‑tense endings.*

---

## 1. What is the Imperfect Indicative Active?

The **imperfect** is a past‑tense mood that describes an **ongoing, repeated, or habitual** action in the past – “was doing”, “used to do”, “did repeatedly”.  

- **Indicative** = the ordinary statement of fact (as opposed to subjunctive, optative, etc.).  
- **Active** = the subject performs the action (as opposed to middle or passive).  

In Ancient Greek the imperfect active is built from three pieces:

| Piece | Position in the verb | Example (λῡ‑ “to loosen”) |
|------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **Augment** | Prefixed to the beginning of the stem (usually **ε‑**) | **ἔ**‑ |
| **Stem + thematic vowel** | The verb’s present stem plus **‑ε‑** (or **‑ι‑** for some verbs) | λῡ‑**ε**‑ |
| **Personal ending** | Same endings as the present active, but the **‑ο‑** of the present is replaced by **‑ον** in the 1st & 3rd persons singular | **‑ον**, **‑ες**, **‑ε(ν)**, **‑ομεν**, **‑ετε**, **‑ον** |

Putting the pieces together gives the classic **‑ω**‑type appearance in the 1st‑person singular of many imperfects (e.g., *ἔλυ‑ον* → “I was loosening”). The **‑ω** you see in the heading refers to the **‑ω**‑type theme vowel that appears in the present, not to the imperfect ending itself.

## 2. Forms and Patterns  

Below is the **full paradigm** for a regular **‑εω** contract verb (e.g., *φιλέω* “to love”). The same pattern works for non‑contract verbs (e.g., *λύω* “to loosen”) after you replace the contract theme vowel **‑ε‑** with the regular **‑ε‑** of the stem.

### 2.1 Regular (non‑contract) verbs – example **λύω**  

| Person | Ending | Form | Gloss |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| 1st sg | **‑ον** | **ἔλυον** | I was loosening |
| 2nd sg | **‑ες** | **ἔλυες** | you were loosening |
| 3rd sg | **‑ε(ν)** | **ἔλυε(ν)** | he/she/it was loosening |
| 1st pl | **‑ομεν** | **ἔλυομεν** | we were loosening |
| 2nd pl | **‑ετε** | **ἔλυετε** | you (pl.) were loosening |
| 3rd pl | **‑ον** | **ἔλυον** | they were loosening |

> **Note:** The augment **ἔ‑** appears only once, at the beginning of the verb, even when the verb is prefixed (e.g., *ἀν‑* → *ἤν* + …).

### 2.2 Contract verbs – example **φιλέω**  

| Person | Ending | Form | Gloss |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| 1st sg | **‑ον** | **ἔφιλον** | I was loving |
| 2nd sg | **‑ες** | **ἔφιλες** | you were loving |
| 3rd sg | **‑ε(ν)** | **ἔφιλε(ν)** | he/she/it was loving |
| 1st pl | **‑ομεν** | **ἔφιλομεν** | we were loving |
| 2nd pl | **‑ετε** | **ἔφιλετε** | you (pl.) were loving |
| 3rd pl | **‑ον** | **ἔφιλον** | they were loving |

### 2.3 Verbs with **‑ι‑** thematic vowel (e.g., *ἔρχομαι* → *ἦλθον* is a different verb, but for imperfect of *πίνω* “to drink”)  

| Person | Ending | Form | Gloss |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| 1st sg | **‑ον** | **ἔπινον** | I was drinking |
| 2nd sg | **‑ες** | **ἔπινες** | you were drinking |
| 3rd sg | **‑ε(ν)** | **ἔπινε(ν)** | he/she/it was drinking |
| 1st pl | **‑ομεν** | **ἔπινομεν** | we were drinking |
| 2nd pl | **‑ετε** | **ἔπινετε** | you (pl.) were drinking |
| 3rd pl | **‑ον** | **ἔπινον** | they were drinking |

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **3.1 Augment** | Add **ε‑** (or **η‑** after a vowel‑initial stem) to the beginning of the verb. | *λύω* → **ἔ**λυ‑; *ἀκούω* → **ἤ**κου‑ (because the stem begins with a vowel). |
| **3.2 Thematic vowel** | Most verbs use **‑ε‑**; verbs whose present stem ends in **‑ι‑** keep that **‑ι‑** (e.g., *πίνω* → *πιν‑*). | *πίνω* → *πιν‑*; *φιλέω* → *φιλε‑*. |
| **3.3 Personal endings** | Same as present active, but **‑ο‑** → **‑ον** in 1st & 3rd sg. | *λύω* → *ἔλυον*, *ἔλυε(ν)*. |
| **3.4 Contract verbs** | The contract vowel (‑α‑, ‑ε‑, ‑ο‑) merges with the augment, giving forms like **ἔ-** + **α** → **ἤ‑** (e.g., *ἀγαπάω* → *ἤγαγον*? actually *ἠγάπα* is aorist; for imperfect: *ἔ-αγαπ‑ον* → *ἔαγαπον*). | *φιλέω* → **ἔφιλον**. |
| **3.5 Irregular verbs** | Some very common verbs have special stems: <br>• **εἰμί** → *ἦν, ἦσθα, ἦν, ἦμεν, ἦτε, ἦσαν* (no augment, no **‑ε‑**). <br>• **ἔχω** → *ἔεχον* (double epsilon). <br>• **ἔρχομαι** → *ἦλθον* (different root). | See examples below. |
| **3.6 Double augment** | When a verb already begins with **ε‑** (or **η‑**), the augment is **ἠ‑** (long vowel). | *ἔρχομαι* → **ἦ**ρχομαι → imperfect *ἦρχον*. |
| **3.7 Prefixes** | The augment is placed **after** the prefix, not before it. | *ἀν‑* + *λύω* → **ἤ**ν‑ + *λυ‑* → **ἤνλυον** → *ἤνλυον* (rare; more common: *ἀν‑* + *τίθημι* → *ἔ* + *τιθη‑* → *ἔτιθην*). |

| Greek (Imperfect) | Transliteration | English |
|-------------------|----------------|---------|
| 1. **ἔλυον τὸν δεσμὸν**. | **éluon tòn desmòn**. | I was loosening the bond. |
| 2. **ἔφιλε τὸν παῖδα**. | **éphile tòn paîda**. | He/she was loving the child. |

## 5. Notes on Usage & Context  

1. **Narrative past** – The imperfect is the default past tense for background actions in prose and drama.  
2. **Repeated/habitual** – Use when an action happened repeatedly or habitually: *ἦν ἐν τῇ πόλει ὁ διδάσκαλος* “the teacher used to be in the city”.  
3. **Contrast with Aorist** – The aorist presents a simple, punctual past action; the imperfect stresses the *duration* or *process*.  
4. **Poetic licence** – In epic poetry, the imperfect can appear where prose would use the aorist, especially for vivid, ongoing scenes.  
5. **Verb‑specific quirks** – Some verbs (especially *εἰμί*, *ἔχω*, *ἔρχομαι*) have irregular imperfect stems; treat them as lexical items.

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology  

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Augment origin** | The augment (ε‑) is a relic of Proto‑Indo‑European *e‑* “pre‑past” marker, also seen in the perfect of some Indo‑European languages. |
| **‑ε‑ thematic vowel** | The **‑ε‑** of the imperfect reflects the original *‑e‑* present‑stem vowel of the Indo‑European *‑eh₁‑* suffix used for continuous aspect. |
| **‑ον ending** | The **‑ον** ending derives from the Proto‑Indo‑European *‑om* (1st sg) and *‑ont* (3rd pl) with a later Greek vowel change. |
| **Contract verbs** | The contraction of the augment with the vowel of the stem (e.g., *ἔ* + *α* → *ἤ*) mirrors a phonological process that began in the Classical period and became regularized. |
| **Irregular stems** | Verbs like *εἰμί* preserve an older *‑s‑* copular root (*es‑*), while *ἔρχομαι* shows a suppletive perfect stem (*ηλθ‑*) that was re‑analysed for the imperfect. |
