# Lesson: The Present Active Participle (‑ων) in Ancient Greek  

*Target level: Beginner‑Intermediate*  

---

## 1. What is the Present Active Participle?

The **present active participle** is a verbal adjective that expresses an **ongoing, simultaneous, or habitual action** performed by the subject of the clause.  

- **Tense**: Present → the action is contemporaneous with the main verb.  
- **Voice**: Active → the subject of the participle is the doer of the action.  
- **Mood**: Participle (non‑finite) → it does not have a finite verb ending; instead it is declined like an adjective.  

In English the present active participle is usually rendered with **‑ing** (e.g., “the **speaking** man”) or with a relative clause (“the man **who is speaking**”).

> **Greek:** ὁ **λαλῶν** ἀνὴρ  
> **English:** “the speaking man” / “the man who is speaking”

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns  

The present active participle is formed from the verb stem plus the thematic vowel **‑ο‑** and the participial suffix **‑ν‑**. The resulting stem ends in **‑ων‑** (masc./neut.) or **‑ουσα** (fem.).  

### 2.1. General Paradigm  

| Case / Number | Masculine (‑ων) | Feminine (‑ουσα) | Neuter (‑ον) |
|---------------|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| **Singular** |                |                  |              |
| Nominative    | ‑ων            | ‑ουσα            | ‑ον          |
| Genitive      | ‑οντος         | ‑ουσης           | ‑οντος       |
| Dative        | ‑οντι          | ‑ουσῃ            | ‑οντι        |
| Accusative    | ‑οντα          | ‑ουσαν           | ‑ον          |
| Vocative      | ‑ων            | ‑ουσα            | ‑ον          |
| **Plural**    |                |                  |              |
| Nominative    | ‑οντες         | ‑ουσαι           | ‑οντα        |
| Genitive      | ‑οντων         | ‑ουσῶν           | ‑οντων       |
| Dative        | ‑ουσι(ν)       | ‑ουσῖν           | ‑ουσι(ν)     |
| Accusative    | ‑οντας         | ‑ουσας           | ‑οντα        |
| Vocative      | ‑οντες         | ‑ουσαι           | ‑οντα        |

> **Note:** The dative plural may appear as **‑ουσι** (without the nu) in poetry or early prose; the nu is added before a vowel or for euphony.

### 2.2. Example: The Verb **λύω** “to loose / release”

| Case / Number | Masculine (λύων) | Feminine (λύουσα) | Neuter (λύον) |
|---------------|------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Singular** |                  |                   |              |
| Nominative    | λύων             | λύουσα            | λύον         |
| Genitive      | λύοντος          | λύουσης           | λύοντος      |
| Dative        | λύοντι           | λύουσῃ            | λύοντι       |
| Accusative    | λύοντα           | λύουσαν           | λύον         |
| Vocative      | λύων             | λύουσα            | λύον         |
| **Plural**    |                  |                   |              |
| Nominative    | λύοντες          | λύουσαι           | λύοντα       |
| Genitive      | λύοντων          | λύουσῶν           | λύοντων      |
| Dative        | λύουσι(ν)         | λύουσῖν           | λύουσι(ν)     |
| Accusative    | λύοντας          | λύουσας           | λύοντα       |
| Vocative      | λύοντες          | λύουσαι           | λύοντα       |

### 2.3. Contract Verbs  

Verbs whose stems end in a vowel contract with the thematic vowel **‑ο‑**. The participle then shows a **contraction**:

| Verb (infinitive) | Stem | Present Active Participle (Masc.) |
|-------------------|------|-----------------------------------|
| **φιλεῖν** “to love” | φιλε‑ | **φιλῶν** |
| **πράττειν** “to do” | πράττε‑ | **πράττων** |
| **ἔχειν** “to have” | ἔχε‑ | **ἔχων** |

The feminine and neuter forms follow the same pattern (‑ουσα, ‑ον) after the contracted stem.

### 2.4. Irregular Participles  

Some verbs have **suppletive** or **irregular** present active participles:

| Verb (infinitive) | Regular participle? | Irregular participle |
|-------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **εἰμί** “to be”   | —                   | **ὤν** (masc./neut.), **ὦσα** (fem.) |
| **ἔρχομαι** “to come” | —               | **ἐρχόμενος** (regular) – note the extra **‑με‑** from the middle voice stem |
| **γίγνομαι** “to become” | —            | **γινόμενος** (regular) |

These irregular forms must be memorised.

## 3. Key Rules & Exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. Formation** | Add **‑ων‑** (masc./neut.) or **‑ουσα** (fem.) to the present stem + thematic **‑ο‑**. | λύ‑ + ο + ν → λύων |
| **2. Contract verbs** | Vowel‑ending stems contract with **‑ο‑** (e.g., **φιλε‑** → **φιλ‑** → **φιλῶν**). | ἀγ‑ (ἀγ‑ω) → ἀγῶν → **ἀγῶν** (rare) |
| **3. Accent** | The accent falls on the **penult** if it is long; otherwise on the **antepenult**. The participle follows the same accent rules as any long‑vowel adjective. | λύ‑ **ω**‑ν → **λύ**‑ων (penult long) |
| **4. Gender agreement** | The participle must agree with the noun it modifies in **case, number, gender**. | ὁ **λαλῶν** ἀνὴρ (masc. nom. sg.) |
| **5. Use with the verb “εἰμί”** | The present active participle can be used predicatively with **εἰμί** to express a present state: **εἰμὶ ὁ λύων** “I am the one who is loosing”. | — |
| **6. Exception – εἰμί** | The participle of **εἰμί** is **ὤν, ὦσα, ὄν** (suppletive). | ὁ **ὤν** ἀνήρ – “the man who is”. |
| **7. Dative plural nu‑insertion** | In prose the dative plural often adds a **ν** before a vowel (‑ουσιν). Poetry may omit it. | λύουσιν → λύουσι (poetic) |
| **8. Participles of verbs with a **‑ε‑** in the stem** | The **‑ε‑** often drops before the **‑ων‑** suffix: **πράττω** → **πράττων** (the **ε** disappears). | — |

## 4. Example Sentences  

| Greek (with transliteration) | Translation |
|------------------------------|-------------|
| **Ὁ λύων τὸν δεσμὸν ἀποδέχεται.** <br> *Ho lúōn tòn desmòn apodécheta.* | “The man who is loosening the bond releases it.” |
| **Ἡ φιλῶσα γυνὴ ἀγαπᾷ τὸν ἄνθρωπον.** <br> *Hē philôsa gynē agapâi tòn ánthrōpon.* | “The woman who loves the man loves him.” |
| **Τὰ παιδία τὰ παιδιῶντα ἐν τῷ κῆπῳ εἰσίν.** <br> *Ta paidiá ta paidiônta en tôi kêpôi eisin.* | “The children who are playing in the garden are (there).” |
| **Ὁ ὤν ἐν τῇ οἰκίᾳ ἐστὶν σοφός.** <br> *Ho ōn en tēi oikíā estìn sophós.* | “He who is (present) in the house is wise.” (illustrates the irregular participle of *εἰμί*) |

## 5. Usage & Context  

1. **Attributive use** – placed directly before or after a noun, agreeing in case, number, gender.  
   - *ὁ ἀνὴρ τρέχων* “the running man”.

2. **Predicative use** – often with **εἰμί** or as a **supplement** to a verb of saying, thinking, etc.  
   - *Ἀκούω τὸν ἀνθρώπον λαλῶν* “I hear the man speaking”.

3. **Circumstantial (adverbial) use** – the participle can express **time, cause, condition, concession**.  
   - *Ἔρχεται ὁ ἄνθρωπος ἰδὼν τὸν πόλεμον* “The man comes, seeing the war (i.e., when he sees the war).”

4. **With the article** – the article can be used with the participle to give it a **definite** sense, especially in narrative.  
   - *Ὁ ἀνὴρ ὁ λύων τὸν δεσμὸν* “the man who is loosening the bond”.

5. **In poetry** – the participle may appear **without the article** and often in **shortened forms** (e.g., **ἔχων** → **ἔχον**).  

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology  

- The present active participle derives from the Proto‑Indo‑European *‑nt‑* participial suffix, which also gave the Latin **‑ns** (e.g., *amans* “loving”).  
- In early Greek (Homeric), the participle appears with the **‑ων** ending but often without a distinct article, functioning more like a **verbal noun**.  
- The **‑ων** form is the continuation of the PIE *‑nt‑* participle, while the **‑ουσα / ‑ον** feminine and neuter endings reflect the regular adjective declension patterns inherited from PIE.  
- The irregular participle **ὤν** of *εἰμί* is a suppletive form that goes back to a different PIE root (*h₁es‑* “to be”), showing how the verb “to be” has always been irregular in Indo‑European languages.

## 7. Quick Reference Summary  

| Feature | Present Active Participle |
|---------|---------------------------|
| **Tense** | Present |
| **Voice** | Active |
| **Mood** | Participle (non‑finite) |
| **Typical endings** | Masculine/Neuter **‑ων**, Feminine **‑ουσα** (plus regular adjective declension) |
| **Key uses** | Attributive adjective, circumstantial clause, predicative with *εἰμί* |
| **Irregularities** | *εἰμί* → **ὤν, ὦσα, ὄν**; contract verbs contract before **‑ων‑**; some verbs drop a vowel before **‑ων‑** (πράττω → **πράττων**) |
| **Accent** | Follows normal adjective accent rules (penult long → accent on penult) |
