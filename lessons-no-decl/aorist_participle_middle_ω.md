# Lesson: The Aorist Middle Participle (‑ω)  

*Target level: Beginner → Intermediate*  
*Prerequisites: Greek alphabet, basic noun declension, present‑active‑middle‑passive endings, the concept of voice.*

---

## 1. What is the Aorist Middle Participle?

A **participle** is a verbal adjective – it looks like an adjective but retains the meaning of a verb.  
The **aorist** expresses a *simple, completed* action, without reference to duration.  

The **middle voice** indicates that the subject is acting *for its own benefit* or *in a reflexive/reciprocal* way. In many verbs the middle and passive share the same form; the aorist middle participle is therefore formally identical to the aorist **passive** participle, but its **meaning** is middle.

> **Why it matters** – The aorist middle participle lets you describe a *completed* action that the subject performed *for itself* (or in a deponent sense) and can be used in periphrastic constructions with **εἰμί** (“to be”).

---

## 2. Formation: Stem + Endings  

1. **Find the aorist middle (or passive) stem** of the verb.  
   - For regular verbs the aorist middle stem is usually the same as the aorist passive stem (the one that appears in the aorist passive forms).  
   - For **deponent** verbs the aorist middle participle is the only aorist participle they have.

2. **Add the participial endings** (identical to the aorist passive participle):

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | ‑ων | ‑ουσα | ‑ον |
| Genitive   | ‑ου | ‑ουσῆς | ‑ου |
| Dative     | ‑ῳ | ‑ῳ | ‑ῳ |
| Accusative | ‑ον | ‑ουσαν | ‑ον |
| Vocative   | ‑ων | ‑ουσα | ‑ον |

*The ending **‑ω** you see in the syllabus title is the **masculine nominative singular** ending **‑ων** (pronounced *‑ōn*).*

### Example: λύω “to loose, release”

| Form | Translation (literal) |
|------|-----------------------|
| **λύων** (masc. nom. sg.) | “having loosed (for himself)” |
| **λύουσα** (fem. nom. sg.) | “having loosed (for herself)” |
| **λύον** (neut. nom./acc. sg.) | “having loosed (for itself)” |
| **λύου** (masc./neut. gen. sg.) | “of having loosed” |
| **λύῳ** (dat. sg.) | “to/for having loosed” |
| **λύον** (acc. sg.) | “having loosed” |
| … and the same endings in the plural (‑οντες, ‑ουσαι, ‑οντα, etc.) |

> **Note:** The aorist middle participle of λύω is formally the same as its aorist passive participle. Context (or the verb’s deponent nature) tells us whether the meaning is middle or passive.
