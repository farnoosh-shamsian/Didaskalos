# Lesson: The Present Infinitive – Middle/Passive (‑εσθαι)  

*Target level: Beginner‑Intermediate*  
*Prerequisite knowledge: Greek alphabet, basic verb stems, present indicative active endings*  

---

## 1. What is the Present Infinitive?

The **infinitive** is the “to‑verb” form that expresses the idea of an action without tying it to a particular person, number, or tense.  
In Ancient Greek the present infinitive denotes an **ongoing or habitual** action (as opposed to aorist – a simple, completed action).

Greek has **three voices** for the infinitive:

| Voice | Infinitive ending | Example (from λύω “to loose”) |
|-------|-------------------|--------------------------------|
| Active | ‑ειν | λύειν “to loose” |
| **Middle / Passive** | **‑εσθαι** (or ‑σθαι after a long vowel/diphthong) | **λύεσθαι** “to be loosed / to loose oneself” |
| Deponent (only middle/passive) | ‑εσθαι / ‑σθαι | ἔρχομαι → ἔρχεσθαι “to come (for oneself)” |

The **present infinitive middle/passive** is the focus of this lesson. It is used when the action is **reflexive, reciprocal, or undergoes the action** (passive).  

> **Note:** The present infinitive middle/passive is **not** the same as the present indicative middle/passive ending **‑ω** (first‑person singular). The “(ω)” in the lesson title simply reminds us that the middle/passive present uses the vowel **ω** in the first‑person singular of the indicative (e.g., λύομαι → λύω).  

---

## 2. Forming the Present Infinitive Middle/Passive  

### 2.1 Basic Pattern  

1. **Take the present stem** (the same stem used for the present indicative).  
2. **Add the connective vowel** **‑ε‑** (or **‑ο‑** after a long vowel/diphthong).  
3. **Add the middle/passive infinitive suffix** **‑σθαι**.  

Result: **stem‑ε‑σθαι** → **‑εσθαι** (or **‑σθαι** after contraction).  

### 2.2 Contraction Rules  

| Situation | Resulting infinitive |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Stem ends in **‑α** (short) + **‑ε‑σθαι** → **‑ασθαι** (contracted) | λύα + εσθαι → **λύασθαι** (rare; more common λύεσθαι) |
| Stem ends in **‑α** (long) or **‑η**/**‑ω** → **‑α‑σθαι** contracts to **‑ασθαι** (no extra vowel) | τιμᾶ + σθαι → **τιμᾶσθαι** |
| Stem ends in **‑ε** → **‑εσθαι** contracts to **‑εσθαι** (no change) | λύε + σθαι → **λύεσθαι** |
| Stem ends in **‑ο** → **‑οσθαι** contracts to **‑οσθαι** (no change) | δοῦ → **δοῦσθαι** (from δοῦν) |
| After a diphthong **‑αι, ‑ει, ‑οι, ‑υι** → **‑σθαι** (no **‑ε‑**) | ἀκούει + σθαι → **ἀκούεσθαι** (actually ἀκούεσθαι) |

### 2.3 Regular Paradigm (verb **λῡ́ω** “to loose”)  

| Voice / Mood | Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|--------------|------|----------------|-------|
| **Present Infinitive Active** | λῡ́ειν | *lūein* | “to loose” |
| **Present Infinitive Middle/Passive** | **λῡ́εσθαι** | *lūesthai* | “to be loosed / to loose oneself” |
| **Present Indicative Active** | λῡ́ω, λῡ́εις, λῡ́ει, λῡ́ομεν, λῡ́ετε, λῡ́ουσι(ν) | *lūō …* | “I loose, you loose, …” |
| **Present Indicative Middle** | λῡ́ομαι, λῡ́ει, λῡ́εται, λῡ́ομεθα, λῡ́εσθε, λῡ́ονται | *lūomai …* | “I loose myself, …” |
| **Present Indicative Passive** | λῡ́ομαι, λῡ́ει, λῡ́εται, λῡ́ομεθα, λῡ́εσθε, λῡ́ονται | *lūomai …* | “I am loosed, …” |

> The middle and passive present indicative share the same forms in the **‑ω** series (first‑person singular **λῡ́ω** → **λῡ́ομαι**).

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Add ‑ε‑ before ‑σθαι** | Standard formation for most verbs. | γράφ‑ε‑σθαι → **γράφεσθαι** “to be written / to write oneself” |
| **Contraction after long vowels** | Long α, η, ω → drop the **‑ε‑**; the suffix becomes **‑σθαι** directly. | τιμᾶ‑σθαι → **τιμᾶσθαι** |
| **Verbs ending in ‑ε‑ (contract verbs)** | The **‑ε‑** of the stem merges with the connective **‑ε‑** → single **‑ε‑**. | ἀκού‑ε‑σθαι → **ἀκούεσθαι** |
| **Deponent verbs** | Have only middle/passive forms; their infinitive is middle/passive. | ἔρχομαι → **ἔρχεσθαι** “to come (for oneself)” |
| **Irregular infinitives** | Some common verbs have irregular stems. | εἰμί → **εἶναι** (active only); ὁράω → **ὁρᾶν** (active) but middle/passive **ὁρᾶσθαι** |
| **No middle/passive infinitive for some verbs** | Verbs that lack a middle/passive voice (e.g., *ἔρχομαι* has only middle) simply use the existing form. | — |
| **Future infinitive** | Not covered here, but note that the future infinitive uses **‑ειν** for all voices (e.g., λῡ́σειν). | — |

## 4. Example Sentences  

| Greek (with transliteration) | Translation |
|------------------------------|-------------|
| **Βούλομαι λῡ́εσθαι τὸν δεσμὸν.**<br>*Boulomai lūesthai ton desmon.* | “I want to be loosed from the bond.” |
| **Ἀκούει ἀκούεσθαι τὸν λόγον.**<br>*Akoue akouesthai ton logon.* | “He hears the word being heard (i.e., he hears the word spoken).” |
| **Διδάσκειν ἐστὶν ἀγαθὸν, ἀλλ’ ἐμαθόντες ἀσκοῦνται.**<br>*Didáskein estin agathon, all’ emathóntes askountai.* | “Teaching is good, but those who have learned are ashamed (they are ashamed of having been taught).” |
| **Ἐπιδέχομαι τὸν ἥλιον ἀνατέλλειν.**<br>*Epidéchomai ton hēlion anatellein.* | “I endure the sunrise (literally, I endure the sun rising).” |

*Notice how the infinitive middle/passive (‑εσθαι) follows verbs of desire, perception, or purpose.*

---

## 5. Usage & Context  

1. **After verbs of will, desire, or command** – *βούλομαι, ἐθέλω, ἀποτρέπω* → infinitive expresses the intended action.  
2. **After verbs of perception** – *ὁρῶ, ἀκούω, αἰσθάνομαι* – the middle/passive infinitive can indicate that the subject experiences the action upon itself.  
3. **In purpose clauses** – often introduced by *ἵνα* or *ὡς*; the infinitive can replace a finite verb: *ἔρχεται ἵνα λῡ́εσθαι* “he comes in order to be loosed.”  
4. **With deponent verbs** – the infinitive is always middle/passive, never active.  
5. **In indirect speech** – the infinitive can stand for a finite verb when the subject is the same as the main clause’s subject.  

---

## 6. Historical Development & Etymology  

- The **‑σθαι** suffix goes back to Proto‑Indo‑European *‑s‑* (reflexive) + *‑th₂* (passive voice marker).  
- The connective vowel **‑ε‑** (or **‑ο‑**) is a relic of the original *‑e‑* that linked the verb stem to the suffix.  
- In **Proto‑Greek**, the middle and passive voices were not fully distinct; they merged into a single **‑σθαι** infinitive.  
- The contraction patterns (‑α + ‑εσθαι → ‑ασθαι) reflect early Greek vowel‑length alternations that later became regularized in the Attic dialect.  
- The **deponent** phenomenon (verbs that lack an active form) likely arose from the loss of the active voice in certain semantic fields (e.g., *ἔρχομαι* “to come” originally had an active *ἔρχω* that disappeared).
