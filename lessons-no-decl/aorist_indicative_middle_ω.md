# Lesson: The Aorist Indicative Middle (ending ‑ω)

---

## 1. What the Aorist Indicative Middle Is  

| Term | Meaning in Greek | English Approximation |
|------|------------------|-----------------------|
| **Aorist** | A “simple past” aspect – it presents an action as a single, completed event, without reference to its duration or repetition. | “He/She/It **did** …” |
| **Indicative** | The ordinary mood of statement, used for facts, questions, or ordinary narration. | “He **went** …” |
| **Middle** | A voice that shows the subject acting **for his own benefit**, **with a reflexive nuance**, or **in a state of being**. It often corresponds to English “‑self” or “‑for oneself”, but many middle uses have no direct English equivalent. | “He **washed** himself.” / “He **took** (the opportunity).” |

When these three categories combine we get a verb form that:

* tells us **when** the action happened (aorist = past, simple),
* tells us **that it really happened** (indicative = factual),
* tells us **how the subject is involved** (middle = self‑directed, reflexive, or “for his own sake”).

The **‑ω** you see in the paradigm is the **second‑person singular middle ending** of the aorist indicative. It is pronounced *‑sō* (the sigma of the aorist augment is lost in the 2nd sg middle).

---

## 2. Forms and Patterns  

### 2.1. The Aorist Middle Endings  

| Person / Number | Middle Ending | Example with **λῦσις** (to loose) → **ἐλυσάμην** (I loosed) |
|-----------------|---------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1st sg | **‑σάμην** | ἐλυσάμην |
| 2nd sg | **‑σω** | ἐλυσῇ (often written **‑σω**) |
| 3rd sg | **‑σάτο** | ἐλυσάτο |
| 1st pl | **‑σάμεθα** | ἐλυσάμεθα |
| 2nd pl | **‑σάσθε** | ἐλυσάσθε |
| 3rd pl | **‑σαντο** | ἐλυσάντο |

> **Note:** The augment **ἐ‑** (or **ἠ‑** after a vowel) is added to the stem before the ending, just as in the aorist active. The augment is **not** added to the 2nd sg middle because the ending already begins with a vowel (‑σω); the augment would give **‑ε‑σω**, which is not used.

### 2.2. Full Paradigm (Regular Thematic –ω verb)  

We will use the verb **τιμάω** “to honor” (the aorist middle is **ἐτιμήσομαι**).  

| Person / Number | Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|-----------------|------|----------------|-------|
| 1st sg | **ἐτιμήσάμην** | etimēsámēn | “I honored (for myself / in my own interest)” |
| 2nd sg | **ἐτιμήσω** | etimēsō | “you honored (for yourself)” |
| 3rd sg | **ἐτιμήσάτο** | etimēsáto | “he/she/it honored (for himself/herself)” |
| 1st pl | **ἐτιμήσάμεθα** | etimēsámetha | “we honored (for ourselves)” |
| 2nd pl | **ἐτιμήσάσθε** | etimēsásthe | “you (pl.) honored (for yourselves)” |
| 3rd pl | **ἐτιμήσαντο** | etimésanto | “they honored (for themselves)” |

### 2.3. Contract Verbs  

For verbs whose stem ends in a vowel, the aorist middle often contracts:

| Verb (infinitive) | Aorist middle 1st sg | 2nd sg (‑ω) | 3rd sg |
|-------------------|----------------------|------------|--------|
| **φιλέω** “to love” | ἐφίλησα (active) → **ἐφίλησάμην** (middle) | **ἐφίλησας** → **ἐφίλησας** (active) – middle **‑σω** gives **ἐφίλησας**? Actually middle 2sg = **ἐφίλησας**? Wait, contract: **ἐφίλησας** is active; middle 2sg = **ἐφίλησας**? The middle 2sg ending is **‑σω**, so **ἐφίλησας** → **ἐφίλησας**? Better choose a clearer example: **διδάσκειν** → aorist middle **ἐδιδάσκομαι** (1sg) and **ἐδιδάσῃ** (2sg) → **‑σω** becomes **‑σῃ** after contraction. |
| **παιδεύω** “to educate” | **ἐπαιδεύσάμην** | **ἐπαιδεύσω** | **ἐπαιδεύσάτο** |

*The table shows that the 2nd sg middle ending **‑σω** may appear as **‑σῃ** after contraction (e.g., **ἐδιδάσῃ**).*

## 3. Key Rules & Common Exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **A. Augment** – Add **ἐ‑** (or **ἠ‑** after a vowel) to the aorist stem before the ending, **except** in the 2nd sg middle. | The augment marks past time. | **ἐ‑λυσ‑** + **‑σάμην** → **ἐλυσάμην** |
| **B. Middle endings contain a sigma (‑σ‑)** – The sigma is part of the middle voice marker. | The sigma appears in all middle endings (‑σάμην, ‑σω, ‑σάτο, …). | **ἐ‑τιμή‑σάμην** |
| **C. Contract verbs** – When the stem ends in a vowel, the sigma may cause contraction, giving **‑σῃ**, **‑σά**, etc. | **διδάσκομαι** → **ἐδιδάσῃ** (2sg) | |
| **D. Verbs with a **‑ε‑** or **‑ο‑** aorist stem** – The middle endings are attached to that stem, not to the present stem. | **πράττω** → aorist stem **πρᾱξ‑** → middle **πρᾱξάμην** | |
| **E. Irregular middle aorists** – Some verbs have a **different** aorist middle form (often the same as the passive). | **ἔρχομαι** (present) → aorist middle **ἦλθον** (active) but middle **ἦλθον** is also used; the verb **ἔρχομαι** lacks a distinct middle aorist. | |
| **F. The “‑ω” ending** – In the 2nd sg middle, the ending is **‑σω** (pronounced *‑sō*). It looks like the present middle **‑ῃ** after contraction, but it is a distinct aorist form. | **ἐλυσῇ** (2sg middle) = “you loosed (for yourself)”. | |
