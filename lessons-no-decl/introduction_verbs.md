# Lesson: Introduction to Greek verbs

## 1. What Makes Greek Verbs Unique?

Ancient Greek verbs are highly inflected, meaning a single verb form
encodes: - Person - Number - Tense (aspect) - Voice - Mood

## 2. The Five Key Components

### Person & Number

1st (I/we), 2nd (you), 3rd (he/she/it/they)

### Tense (Aspect-Focused)

- Present: ongoing
- Imperfect: past ongoing
- Future: future
- Aorist: simple/completed
- Perfect: completed with result
- Pluperfect: past completed result

### Voice

- Active: subject acts
- Middle: subject acts for itself
- Passive: subject receives action

### Mood

- Indicative: fact
- Subjunctive: possibility/purpose
- Optative: wish/potential
- Imperative: command
- Infinitive: "to do"
- Participle: "doing"

### Principal Parts

6 forms used to build all tenses.

## 3. Basic Verb Anatomy

\[Stem\] + \[Tense Marker\] + \[Ending\]

## Summary

Greek verbs encode who, how, and what kind of action in one word.
