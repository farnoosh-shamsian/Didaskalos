# Lesson: The Present Middle/Passive Participle (‑ω)  

*Target level: Beginner‑Intermediate*  
*Prerequisite knowledge: Greek alphabet, basic noun declension, present indicative active forms.*

---

## 1. What the Present Middle/Passive Participle Is  

A **participle** is a verb form that functions like an adjective or adverb.  
The **present** participle describes an action that is **ongoing or simultaneous** with the main verb.  

The **middle/passive** voice shows that the subject is either:

* **Middle** – acting for its own benefit or in a reflexive way (e.g., “he washes **himself**”), or  
* **Passive** – receiving the action (e.g., “the city is **defended**”).

In Ancient Greek the present middle/passive participle is marked by the **‑ω** theme (‑ο‑) and the characteristic ending **‑όμενος** (masc.), **‑ομένη** (fem.), **‑ον** (neut.).  

> **λῡ́ω** “I loose” → **λῡ́ομαι** “I am loosed / I loose for myself” →  
> **λῡ́ομενος** “being loosed / loosing for himself”

The participle is declined like a **third‑declension adjective** (‑ος/‑η/‑ον).  

---

## 2. Forming the Present Middle/Passive Participle  

### 2.1. Basic steps  

1. **Find the present stem** of the verb (the same stem used for the present indicative).  
   * Example: λῡ‑ (from λῡ́ω)  
2. **Add the thematic vowel ‑ο‑** (sometimes contracted).  
3. **Add the participial suffix ‑μεν‑**.  
4. **Attach the gender‑case endings** of a third‑declension adjective.  

The resulting stem is **‑ομεν‑** (or a contracted form).  

### 2.2. Regular (non‑contract) verbs  

| Verb (present) | Present stem | Middle/Passive participle stem | Example (masc. nom. sg.) |
|----------------|--------------|--------------------------------|--------------------------|
| λῡ́ω “to loose” | λῡ‑ | λῡ‑ + ο + μεν → **λῡομεν‑** | **λῡόμενος** |
| ποιέω “to do” | ποιε‑ | ποιε‑ + ο + μεν → **ποιεομεν‑** | **ποιεόμενος** |
| γράφω “to write” | γραφ‑ | γραφ‑ + ο + μεν → **γραφομεν‑** | **γραφόμενος** |

### 2.3. Contract verbs (‑αω, ‑εω, ‑οω)  

The thematic vowel contracts with the preceding vowel:

| Verb | Present stem | Contracted participle stem | Masculine Nom. sg. |
|------|--------------|---------------------------|--------------------|
| τιμάω “to honor” | τιμα‑ | **τιμ‑** + **ω** → **τιμῶ** + **μεν** → **τιμῶμεν‑** | **τιμῶμενος** |
| φιλέω “to love” | φιλε‑ | **φιλ‑** + **ω** → **φιλῶ** + **μεν** → **φιλῶμεν‑** | **φιλῶμενος** |
| δηλόω “to show” | δηλο‑ | **δηλ‑** + **ω** → **δηλῶ** + **μεν** → **δηλῶμεν‑** | **δηλῶμενος** |

*The contraction yields a long vowel (‑α → ‑ᾶ, ‑ε → ‑ε͂, ‑ο → ‑ο͂) before ‑μεν.*

### 2.4. Irregularities & gaps  

* **εἰμί** “to be” has no present middle/passive participle.  
* Some verbs have suppletive or irregular participles (e.g., **ἔρχομαι** → **ἐρχόμενος** is regular, but **ἔρχομαι** also has the perfect **ἐληλυθώς**).  

---

## 3. Full Paradigm (Third‑Declension Pattern)  

Below is the complete case‑by‑case table for a **regular** present middle/passive participle.  
We use **λῡόμενος** “being loosed / loosing for himself” as the model.

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Nominative** | λῡόμενος | λῡομένη | λῡον |
| **Genitive** | λῡομένου | λῡομένης | λῡομένου |
| **Dative** | λῡομένῳ | λῡομένῃ | λῡομένῳ |
| **Accusative** | λῡόμενον | λῡομένην | λῡον |
| **Vocative** | λῡόμενε | λῡομένη | λῡον |

*The same endings are used for any present middle/passive participle, whatever the verb root.*  

### 3.1. Quick reference table (all genders together)

| Gender | Nom. | Gen. | Dat. | Acc. | Voc. |
|--------|------|------|------|------|------|
| **Masculine** | ‑ **όμενος** | ‑ **ομένου** | ‑ **ομένῳ** | ‑ **όμενον** | ‑ **όμενε** |
| **Feminine** | ‑ **ομένη** | ‑ **ομένης** | ‑ **ομένῃ** | ‑ **ομένην** | ‑ **ομένη** |
| **Neuter** | ‑ **ον** | ‑ **ομένου** | ‑ **ομένῳ** | ‑ **ον** | ‑ **ον** |

*Replace the stem (λῡ‑) with the appropriate verb stem.*

## 4. Key Rules & Common Exceptions  

| Rule | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **1. Add ‑ομεν‑ to the present stem** | The participial suffix is always ‑μεν‑ preceded by the thematic vowel ‑ο‑ (or its contracted form). | λῡ‑ → λῡ**ομεν**‑ → λῡόμενος |
| **2. Contract verbs** | When the stem ends in ‑α, ‑ε, or ‑ο, the ‑ο‑ contracts, giving a long vowel before ‑μεν‑. | τιμα‑ → **τιμῶ**μεν‑ → **τιμῶμενος** |
| **3. Decline like a third‑declension adjective** | All case endings follow the regular third‑declension pattern shown above. | see paradigm |
| **4. No participle for εἰμί** | The verb “to be” lacks a present middle/passive participle; use other constructions (e.g., ὢν). | — |
| **5. Irregular stems** | Some verbs have a stem change before the participle (e.g., **ἔρχομαι** → **ἐρχόμενος**; the vowel ε drops). | ἔρχομαι → ἐρχόμενος |
| **6. Semantic restriction** | The middle sense is reflexive or “for oneself”; the passive sense is “being …”. Not all verbs naturally take a middle meaning. | λῡόμενος can mean “being loosed” (passive) or “loosing for himself” (middle). |
| **7. Use with the article** | When the participle functions as a noun, it is usually preceded by the definite article (ὁ λῡόμενος). | ὁ λῡόμενος ἐστὶν ἀνὴρ – “The one who is being loosed is a man.” |

## 5. Example Sentences  

| Greek (with transliteration) | Translation |
|------------------------------|-------------|
| **1.** Ὁ ἀνὴρ **λῡόμενος** ὑπὸ τῶν ἀνδρῶν ἀποδιδράσκει.<br>*(Ho anēr lūómenos hypo tōn andrōn apodidraskei.)* | “The man **being loosed** by the men runs away.” |
| **2.** Ἡ γυνὴ **φιλοῦσα** τὸν ἄνθρωπον ἀγαπᾷ.<br>*(Hē gynē philousa ton anthrōpon agapaí.)* – *Note: this is an **active** participle for contrast.* |
| **3.** Ἐγὼ **παιδεύομαι** ὑπὸ τῶν διδασκάλων.<br>*(Egō paideuomai hypo tōn didaskalōn.)* – “I am **being taught** by the teachers.” |
| **4.** **Τιμῶμενος** ὑπὸ τῶν θεῶν, ὁ ἥρως ἀνέβη εἰς τὸν οὐρανόν.<br>*(Timōmenos hypo tōn theōn, ho hērōs anebē eis ton ouranon.)* | “**Honored** by the gods, the hero ascended to heaven.” |

*In (1) the participle is middle/passive; in (3) the verb itself is middle/passive, showing the same idea.*

---

## 6. Usage & Context  

1. **Descriptive adjective** – Often used with the article to describe a person or thing:  
   *ὁ λῡόμενος δέσμιος* “the one who is being loosed”.  

2. **Circumstantial clause** – Replaces a subordinate clause meaning “while …” or “when …”:  
   *Ἀνέβαινεν ὁ ἥρως, **προστρατεύων** τὸν στρατὸν.* – “The hero was climbing, **while leading** the army.”  

3. **With verbs of perception or cognition** – The participle can express the *state*
