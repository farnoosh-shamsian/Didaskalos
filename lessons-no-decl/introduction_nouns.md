# Ancient Greek Nouns --- Core Structure (Quick Guide)

## 1. What Makes Greek Nouns Unique?

Ancient Greek nouns are highly inflected and change form based on: -
Case - Number - Gender

## 2. The Three Key Components

### Case (Function in Sentence)

- Nominative: subject
- Genitive: possession ("of")
- Dative: indirect object ("to/for")
- Accusative: direct object
- Vocative: address

### Number

- Singular: one
- Plural: many
- Dual (rare): two

### Gender

- Masculine
- Feminine
- Neuter

## 3. Declensions

Nouns follow patterns called declensions:

- 1st Declension: mostly feminine
- 2nd Declension: masculine & neuter
- 3rd Declension: mixed, more complex

## 4. Basic Noun Anatomy

\[Stem\] + \[Case Ending\]

Endings change depending on case and number.

## 5. Why It Matters

Word order is flexible because endings show function.

## Summary

Greek nouns encode role, number, and gender through endings.
